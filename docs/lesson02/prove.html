02 Prove: Validate User Input

Overview

Prove that you can write a Python program that validates user input and uses that input in calculations.

Problem Statement

While entering input for a program, users occasionally make mistakes and enter invalid or incorrect input. A good program recovers from user mistakes, informs users about mistakes in clear polite language, and gives users an opportunity to correct their mistakes.

Helpful Documentation

The code example in this lesson's prepare content shows how to require a user to enter a non-negative integer between a minimum and maximum value.

This short video shows a BYU-Idaho faculty member solving a problem that is similar to this prove assignment.

Assignment

The previous lesson's prove assignment required you to write a program that computes the approximate volume of air inside a tire. Change the program that you wrote for the previous lesson's prove assignment so that it validates all three of the integers that the user enters while using the program. The minimum and maximum for each of the three numbers are as follows:

Quantity Minimum Maximum
tire width 180 400
aspect ratio 45 70
wheel diameter 12 28

Sample Run

> python prove_solution.py
Enter the width of the tire in mm (ex 205): tire
'tire' is not a positive integer. Please enter a positive integer.
Enter the width of the tire in mm (ex 205): 60
60 is too small. Please enter a positive integer between 180 and 400.
Enter the width of the tire in mm (ex 205): 550
550 is too large. Please enter a positive integer between 180 and 400.
Enter the width of the tire in mm (ex 205): 205

Enter the aspect ratio of the tire (ex 60): aspect
'aspect' is not a positive integer. Please enter a positive integer.
Enter the aspect ratio of the tire (ex 60): 15
15 is too small. Please enter a positive integer between 45 and 70.
Enter the aspect ratio of the tire (ex 60): 90
90 is too large. Please enter a positive integer between 45 and 70.
Enter the aspect ratio of the tire (ex 60): 60

Enter the diameter of the tire in inches (ex 15): wheel
'wheel' is not a positive integer. Please enter a positive integer.
Enter the diameter of the tire in inches (ex 15): 10
10 is too small. Please enter a positive integer between 12 and 28.
Enter the diameter of the tire in inches (ex 15): 35
35 is too large. Please enter a positive integer between 12 and 28.
Enter the diameter of the tire in inches (ex 15): 15

The approximate volume is 39924.5 milliliters.

Testing Procedure

Verify that your program works correctly by following each step in this testing procedure:

  1. Run your program and enter the inputs shown in the "Sample Run" section above. Ensure that your program's output matches the sample run output.
