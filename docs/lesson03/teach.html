The Problem

When you physically exercise to strengthen your heart, you should maintain your heart rate within a range. To find that range, subtract your age from 220. This difference is your maximum heart rate per minute. Your heart simply will not beat faster than this maximum (220 − age). When exercising to strengthen your heart, you should keep your heart rate between 65% and 85% of your heart’s maximum.

Instructions

Write a Python program that asks for a person’s age and computes and outputs the slowest and fastest rates necessary to strengthen his heart.

Core Requirements

  1. Your program must compute the slowest and fastest beneficial heart rates for someone trying to strengthen her heart with physical exercise.
  2. If the user enters an invalid age, including any letters, numbers with a decimal place, and a negative number, your program must output "Invalid age" and not perform the calcuations nor output anything else.

Stretch Challenges

  1. If the user enters an age less than 12, your program should print, "The formulas used by this program are not valid for people younger than 12 years-old." (without the quotes), and your program should not perform the calculations nor output anything else.
  2. If the user enters an age greater than 80, your program should change the age to 80 and perform and output the calculations.
